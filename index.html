The Golf Journal

Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy explains how The Golf Journal accesses, stores, uses, and shares data when you use the app.

What The Golf Journal stores

The Golf Journal stores the information you create in the app, including clubs, club photos and attachments, sessions, scorecards, tee times, and notes.

This data is primarily stored locally on your device using the app's on-device database and file storage.

The Golf Journal does not require you to create an account to use the app.

Permissions and features

Location is used when you choose course and weather features that need your position, such as capturing session weather and nearby conditions.

Camera and photo library access are used so you can take or attach photos for clubs and related items.

Notifications are used for tee time reminders that you choose to set up.

Weather and network requests

When weather or location search features are used, The Golf Journal sends the required location or search details to Open-Meteo services to return forecasts, historical weather, and place matches.

Weather attribution and provider details are available in the app’s Licence screen.

Sharing and backups

The Golf Journal does not sell your personal data.

Your app data only leaves your device when you choose to share a backup, restore from a backup file, or use features that request weather and location search results from Open-Meteo.

If you export a backup or share a file, that copy is under your control and handled according to the app, service, or storage location you choose.

Security and permissions

The Golf Journal is designed to keep your data on your device unless you choose to share or restore a backup, or use a feature that sends location or search details to Open-Meteo.

Network requests used by the app are sent over HTTPS, and Android runtime permissions are requested when needed for relevant features such as location, camera, photos, and notifications.

Your control, retention, and deletion

You are in control of your data. You can delete clubs, sessions, tee times, photos, and attachments inside the app at any time.

You can also clear the app database from Settings, which removes the locally stored clubs, sessions, tee times, and shots from the app.

Data created in The Golf Journal remains on your device until you delete it, clear the app database, or remove the app from your device. Backup files you export remain wherever you save or share them until you delete those copies yourself.

Developer contact

If you have a privacy question or want to submit an inquiry about your data, you can contact the developer using the contact details shown on The Golf Journal's Google Play store listing.

That store listing contact point is the support and privacy contact mechanism for this app.
